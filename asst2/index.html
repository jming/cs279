Command Selection Experiment

Participation is voluntary.

It is your choice whether or not to participate in this research. If you choose to participate, you may change your mind and leave the study at any time.

Description of the proposed study.

You will be asked to participate in a command selection task, which will take approximately ten to fifteen minutes. In this task, you will be asked to select the icon related to a corresponding task. Please read the instructions before starting the task, and if anything is unclear about the rules, you may ask before starting. After completing the task, you may ask questions about the purpose of the game and the research goals.

Potential risks and discomforts.

There is minimal risk that you may experience eye strain or other discomforts related to completing a task on a computer screen, but since the duration of the task is short, the probability of experiencing any discomfort is very low. We will be logging data based on your movements and actions during the task, but the data will not be linked to any identifiable information about yourself.

Potential benefits.

There are no special benefits you should expect from participating in the study; however, we hope you will find the experience engaging and enjoyable. The data gained from this study will help advance research in human computer interaction (in the context of artificial intelligence).

Compensation.

You will not be compensated in any monetary form for participating in this research.

Instructions

You will be presented with two types of interfaces. For each interface, you will be asked to complete a series of tasks, each indicated by an image and short description. To complete the task, please click on the button on the interface corresponding with the given task. You will have a practice phase to get familiar with the interface, followed by a testing phase.

Before you begin:

  • Turn your volume up.
  • Make sure your screen is maximized.
